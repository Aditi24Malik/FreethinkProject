Details

The scheme “Financial Assistance to Disabled Students Pursuing (10th, 11th, 12th Equivalent Exams)” was launched by the Department of Social Justice, Government of Kerala. Under the scheme, financial assistance will be provided to such school dropouts to pursue SSLC, +1, +2 equivalent courses implemented by State Literacy Mission.

A person becomes disabled by birth or because of an accident or disease. The differently-abled persons face many hurdles in their day-to-day life. About 90% of disabled persons may not have received even primary education. They are forced to give up studies due to various factors, such as difficulty in going to educational institutions and inadequate road facilities.

Eligibility
  • The applicant should be a resident of Kerala State.
  • The differently abled students with 40% or more disability are eligible to apply under the scheme.
  • Financial assistance will be provided to students falling in Above Poverty Line (APL) as well as Below Poverty Line (BPL) categories.
Application Process

Offline

  1. Step 01: Visit the nearest Anganwadi Centre or District Social Justice Office and collect the application form. The application form can also be downloaded from the official website.
  2. Step 02: Fill out the application form completely.
  3. Step 03: Submit the form to the District Social Justice Officers (DSJOs) or Anganwadi Centre.
  • Note 01: DSJOs must ensure applicants have completed courses and received certificates from the State Literacy Mission.
  • Note 02: Beneficiaries are selected based on applications through Anganwadi workers and CDPOs.
Documents Required
  • Identity proof
  • Passport-size photo
  • Educational Certificates
  • BPL Card (If applicable)
  • Disability Certificate
  • Income Certificate / Ration card
  • Bank account details
Frequently Asked Questions
What is the “Financial Assistance to Disabled Students Pursuing (10th, 11th, 12th Equivalent Exams)” scheme?
This scheme provides financial support to differently-abled students in Kerala to pursue 10th to 12th equivalent courses via the State Literacy Mission.
Which department has launched this scheme?
The Department of Social Justice, Government of Kerala.
Who is eligible to get the benefits under the scheme?
Differently-abled students with 40% or more disability, who are residents of Kerala and belong to either APL or BPL categories.
Which classes students will be eligible to apply under the scheme?
Students appearing for SSLC/10th, 11th, or 12th equivalent exams.
Is this scheme only for differently abled students?
Yes, the scheme is exclusively for students with a minimum of 40% disability.
Is this scheme only for a resident of Kerala?
Yes, the applicant must be a resident of Kerala to be eligible.
